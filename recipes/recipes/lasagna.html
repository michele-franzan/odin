Lasagna

Description

Lasagna is a rich and satisfying baked pasta dish featuring layers of wide pasta sheets, a slow-simmered meat ragù (Bolognese sauce), creamy besciamella sauce, and generous amounts of grated Parmesan cheese. Each layer melds together during baking, creating a comforting and flavorful dish that's perfect for family gatherings or special occasions. This recipe focuses on a traditional preparation for an authentic taste.

Ingredients

For the Bolognese Sauce (Ragù):

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 carrots, finely diced
  • 2 celery stalks, finely diced
  • 1 pound ground beef (80/20 blend recommended)
  • 1/2 pound ground pork (optional, but adds flavor)
  • 1/2 cup dry red wine
  • 1 (28 ounce) can crushed tomatoes
  • 1/2 cup whole milk
  • 1/4 teaspoon grated nutmeg
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste

For the Besciamella Sauce:

  • 4 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 4 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 4 cups whole milk, warmed
  • 1/4 teaspoon grated nutmeg
  • Salt and freshly ground white pepper to taste

For Assembly:

  • 1 pound dried lasagna pasta (not no-boil)
  • 1 1/2 cups grated Parmesan cheese, plus more for serving
  • Fresh basil leaves, for garnish (optional)

Steps

Prepare the Bolognese Sauce (Ragù):

  • Heat the olive oil in a large, heavy-bottomed pot or Dutch oven over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and cook until softened and translucent, about 5-7 minutes.
  • Add the diced carrots and celery and cook for another 5-7 minutes, until slightly tender.
  • Increase the heat to medium-high and add the ground beef and ground pork (if using). Cook, breaking up the meat with a spoon, until browned. Drain off any excess fat.
  • Pour in the red wine and scrape the bottom of the pot to loosen any browned bits. Let the wine simmer and reduce slightly, about 5 minutes.
  • Stir in the crushed tomatoes, whole milk, and nutmeg. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  • Bring the sauce to a gentle simmer, then reduce the heat to low, cover, and cook for at least 2 hours, or up to 3 hours, stirring occasionally. The longer it simmers, the richer the flavor will be. If the sauce becomes too thick, add a little water or beef broth. Adjust seasoning as needed.

Prepare the Besciamella Sauce:

  • In a medium saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat.
  • Whisk in the flour and cook for 1-2 minutes, stirring constantly, to form a smooth roux. Do not let it brown.
  • Gradually whisk in the warm milk, a little at a time, ensuring each addition is fully incorporated and smooth before adding more. Continue whisking constantly to prevent lumps.
  • Bring the sauce to a gentle simmer over medium heat, stirring continuously, until it thickens and coats the back of a spoon, about 5-7 minutes.
  • Remove from the heat and stir in the nutmeg. Season with salt and white pepper to taste. Cover the sauce with plastic wrap directly on the surface to prevent a skin from forming.

Cook the Lasagna Pasta:

  • Bring a large pot of salted water to a rolling boil.
  • Add the lasagna pasta, a few at a time, and cook according to the package directions until al dente (slightly undercooked). Be careful not to overcook them, as they will continue to cook in the oven.
  • Carefully remove the cooked pasta with a slotted spoon or tongs and lay them flat on a lightly oiled baking sheet or clean kitchen towels to prevent them from sticking.

Assemble the Lasagna:

  • Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C).
  • Spread a thin layer of the Bolognese sauce on the bottom of a 9x13 inch baking dish.
  • Arrange a layer of lasagna pasta over the sauce, slightly overlapping if necessary.
  • Spread a layer of the Bolognese sauce over the pasta, followed by a layer of the besciamella sauce. Sprinkle generously with grated Parmesan cheese.
  • Repeat the layers: pasta, Bolognese sauce, besciamella sauce, and Parmesan cheese, until all the ingredients are used. The top layer should be pasta, topped with the remaining besciamella sauce and a generous amount of Parmesan cheese.
  • If desired, dot the top with a few small knobs of butter for extra richness and browning.

Bake the Lasagna:

  • Cover the baking dish tightly with aluminum foil.
  • Bake for 30 minutes.
  • Remove the foil and continue baking for another 15-20 minutes, or until the lasagna is bubbling, the cheese is melted and golden brown, and the internal temperature reaches 165°F (74°C).
  • Let the lasagna rest for at least 15-20 minutes before slicing and serving. This allows the layers to set and makes it easier to serve.
  • Garnish with fresh basil leaves, if desired. Serve hot with extra grated Parmesan cheese on the side.
